The main determinants of health are biological factors such as genetics and physio- regulatory dysfunctions; and socioeconomic factors such as poor housing, and low income; and environmental factors such as the quality of air water and food...not to mention human behaviour or lifestyle factors.

Owing to globalization and human migrations across the 4 corners of the Earth, the exposure to knowledge about true meanings of developed and developing or underdeveloped world have become the reality of the day...and we can all understand that disparities between pre-industrial and industrialized economic systems where demographic and epidemiologic transitions are making big headlines.

Poverty provides nothing but disaster to human happiness and the freedom to express the full human potential and display what one is truly made up of.

Poverty also bears a mentality that can be self- destructive, low self esteem, lacking the ability to create...and over-reliance on utopian realities...with excess wishful thinking!

In health, low income countries are regarded as places where most communicable and non- communicable diseases have their palatial habitations...this is due to lack of access to good healthcare systems and quality medicines by majority of the general populace... These are countries where mental health issues have not yet been given much priority...development of good sanitation and hygiene systems are still in their infancy in the 21st century! Because most people have misplaced priorities..and have less interest in understanding the true reality facing their very existence!
